## 👨‍💻 About me

I am an applied microeconomist working in public policy, urban economics, and household finance. Across these areas, my research focuses on answering relevant applied questions using reduced-form causal inference with observational and experimental data. Examples include: Does rent control provide financial benefits to tenants? How does the rise of corporate landlords affect neighborhood dynamics? How do households substitute between credit, insurance, and other financial tools when managing risk?

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked for over ten years across Argentina’s public sector, private sector, and independent consulting. I held a variety of roles, generally centered on applied econometrics and data analysis. My work included evaluating public policies, as well as designing and implementing forecasting models used to project tax revenues and support fiscal planning.

Why this GitHub? Over time, I realized that one of the best ways to truly learn a topic is to prepare to teach it. Much like preparing lecture notes for a class, I use this repository to develop structured notes and reproducible code on topics I want to understand deeply. Some are areas I already work on but want to explore further; others are subjects I am learning from scratch. The goal is to turn learning into something organized, transparent, and reusable.

Across industry and academia, my goal is the same: to produce evidence and tools that are both statistically rigorous and operationally meaningful.

## 📂 List of repositories:

Most repositories are structured as self-contained modules combining theory, simulations, and reproducible code.

### A) Applied ML

**credit-risk-lendingclub**  
End-to-end machine learning pipeline for credit risk modeling using the LendingClub dataset, including feature engineering, model selection, and explainability tools.

**customer-feedback-NLP**  
NLP toolkit for analyzing customer feedback using embeddings, topic modeling, and transformer-based models.

### B) Causal inference & experimentation

**ab-test**  
Practical framework for designing and analyzing online experiments at scale.

**causal-ai**  
Notes and implementations at the intersection of causal inference and machine learning.

### C) Research notes & foundations

**structural_change**  
Framework for detecting structural breaks, concept drift, and data drift in machine learning systems.

**deep-learning**  
Self-study notes and experiments on neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, and transformers.

**large-language-models**  
Research-oriented notes on LLM foundations, scaling laws, alignment, and inference.

**artificial-intelligence**  
Conceptual notes on AI foundations and emerging research directions.
